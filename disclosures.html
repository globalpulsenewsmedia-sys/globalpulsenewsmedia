Risk Disclosures

SECURITY CLASSIFICATION: PUBLIC EFFECTIVE DATE: MAY 18, 2026

IMPORTANT REGULATORY WARNING: Trading digital assets, leveraging algorithmic signals, supplying liquidity to decentralized protocols, and utilizing high-frequency financial platforms carry immense risk. This Risk Disclosure document outlines the primary risk factors associated with blockchain liquidity pools, algorithmic trade orchestration, and market-moving events.

1. Cryptographic and Volatility Risk

Cryptographic tokens and digital assets are subject to extreme, short-term market fluctuations. Prices can collapse or skyrocket by more than 50% in a single day due to sentiment shifts, developer actions, regulatory adjustments, or market illiquidity. Arbitrage yields and liquidity spreads depend entirely on these market differences, and any sudden stabilization or lockup can wipe out profit margins.

2. Smart Contract and Decentralized Protocol Risk

Decentralized applications, automated market makers (AMMs), and liquidity pools are governed by open-source smart contracts. These contracts are subject to severe vulnerabilities, zero-day exploits, developer backdoors, and logic bugs. Even audited protocols have suffered catastrophic economic hacks and oracle manipulation exploits. By relying on DEX metrics, you recognize that the underlying pools can experience flash-loan attacks or infinite mint vulnerabilities at any second.

3. High-Frequency Execution and Network Latency

Successful cross-market arbitrage requires executing trades in milliseconds. Digital ledger environments are subject to congestion, transaction queue blockages (such as Solana mempool constraints or Ethereum gas spikes), and node disconnects. Latency variations between the moment an arbitrage signal is generated and when it is confirmed can turn a profitable atomic route into a loss-making trade, a phenomenon known as negative slippage.

4. Impermanent Loss

Depositing liquidity into multi-asset pools to farm arbitrage execution yields exposes suppliers to Impermanent Loss (IL). If the price ratio of the deposited assets changes drastically compared to when they were deposited, the net value of your pool position can become lower than if you had simply held the assets in a cold storage wallet.
